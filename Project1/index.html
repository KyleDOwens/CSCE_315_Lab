My Projects

Slide Puzzle Solver

I completed this project in a course offered at A&M called CSCE 462 Microcomputer Systems. The project uses a Raspberry Pi, camera, and an LCD Display. This is a device that is capable of solving a 15-slide puzzle and presenting the solution to the user. The device first takes a picture of the board in its unsolved state. Then, the image is sent to our image processing software, which whill identify the edges of the board and crop the image into 16 smaller piece(1 image for each tile). Then, a convolutional neural network will identify which number is on which tile. This information is passed to the puzzle solving algorithm, which uses a variation of the A* search algorithm to determine a method to solve the puzzle. Finally, the resulting moves will be presented to the user on an LCD Display that they can solve along with. More details about the project can be found in our final report and a demonstration of it working can be seen here or in the video shown on the screen.

Baby Sleep Monitoring

During the summer of 2021 I participated in the SEC Directed Internship put together by Texas A&M. This was a 7-week summer program, where my group was assigned the problem of finding a way to monitor a baby's breathing while it is sleeping without touching the baby. We were tasked with creating a product, working out how it could be produced, estimated the financial gain/loss, and then give a presentation to a team of industry professionals. The product we came up with was a thin pad that you woud put under the baby's matress that would track the baby's breathing rate, and alert the parent if the baby every stopped breathing.
